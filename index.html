Weather Model Info

HRRR High-Resolution Rapid Refresh

Resolution: 3 km • Range: 0–48 hrs • Updates: Hourly • Coverage: Continental US

The highest resolution model available, HRRR excels at capturing local terrain effects, convective initiation, and fine-scale wind patterns. Updated every hour, it provides the freshest short-range forecast.

Accuracy vs. Time: Excellent 0–12 hrs, good 12–24 hrs, degrades significantly after 24 hrs. Best model for same-day planning. Trust it most for the next 6–12 hours.

ECMWF European Centre for Medium-Range Forecasts

Resolution: ~9 km • Range: 0–15 days • Updates: 2x daily (00Z, 12Z) • Coverage: Global

Widely considered the gold standard for medium-range weather forecasting. The ECMWF IFS model consistently outperforms other global models in verification scores, especially beyond day 3.

Accuracy vs. Time: Very good 0–5 days, good 5–10 days, diminishing skill 10–15 days. Generally the most reliable model for planning several days ahead.

GFS Global Forecast System (Seamless)

Resolution: ~13 km • Range: 0–14 days • Updates: 4x daily (00/06/12/18Z) • Coverage: Global

NOAA's primary global model. GFS Seamless blends higher-resolution models for the short range to improve near-term accuracy. Updates more frequently than ECMWF, providing fresher data 4 times per day.

Accuracy vs. Time: Good 0–5 days, moderate 5–10 days, low skill beyond 10 days. Generally slightly behind ECMWF but still very useful. More frequent updates can catch changes faster.

ICON ICOsahedral Non-hydrostatic Model

Resolution: ~13 km global / ~7 km EU • Range: 0–7 days • Updates: 4x daily • Coverage: Global

Germany's DWD weather service model. ICON uses an innovative icosahedral grid that avoids pole singularities. Provides an independent perspective that's valuable when models disagree — if ICON agrees with ECMWF, confidence increases.

Accuracy vs. Time: Good 0–3 days, moderate 3–5 days, drops off beyond 5 days. Comparable to GFS overall, sometimes better for European locations.

NAM North American Mesoscale

Resolution: ~12 km • Range: 0–84 hrs • Updates: 4x daily • Coverage: North America

NOAA's regional mesoscale model for North America. Provides a useful second opinion alongside HRRR for short-range forecasts. Better at resolving frontal boundaries and precipitation patterns than global models.

Accuracy vs. Time: Good 0–2 days, moderate 2–3 days, low skill beyond 3 days. Most useful as a comparison to HRRR in the first 48 hours.

GEFS Ensemble 30 Members

Members: 30 • Range: 0–14 days • Base: GFS

Runs the GFS model 30 times with slightly different initial conditions. The average gives a more stable forecast than any single run. The spread between members indicates confidence — when members agree (green), the forecast is more trustworthy; when they disagree (yellow/red), conditions are uncertain.

ECMWF Ensemble 50 Members

Members: 50 • Range: 0–14 days • Base: ECMWF IFS

The ECMWF ensemble uses 50 perturbed members — the largest major ensemble system. Combined with the superior ECMWF base model, it provides the most reliable probabilistic forecast available. Color coding works the same as GEFS: green = high agreement, red = low agreement.

Tip: When models disagree on timing or intensity, give more weight to HRRR for 0–24 hrs and ECMWF beyond 3 days. Compare at least 2–3 models before making flying decisions. Use the Ensemble view to gauge overall forecast confidence.
